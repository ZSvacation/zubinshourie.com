First Crewed Lunar Mission in 53 Years

Artemis II Crew Safely Returns — Humans Circle the Moon for the First Time Since 1972

NASA's Artemis II crew splashed down safely, completing the first crewed mission to travel around the Moon in more than 50 years. The 10-day mission sent four astronauts on a free-return trajectory around the lunar far side — not a landing, but a critical demonstration that the Orion capsule and Space Launch System can support human deep-space operations. The last time humans traveled this far from Earth was Apollo 17 in December 1972.

The mission's success marks a genuine inflection point in the renewed space race. China has publicly targeted a crewed lunar landing before 2030, and NASA's Artemis III — the actual Moon landing — is now penciled in for late 2026 or early 2027 pending this data. Commercial partners including SpaceX (providing the Starship Human Landing System) and Axiom Space are already deep in prep. For the first time in a generation, Moon missions are a near-term engineering problem, not a distant aspiration.

The cultural moment is hard to overstate: most people alive today have never seen a human travel beyond low Earth orbit. That changed this week.

Today’s Rabbit Hole
Advertising · AI · Commerce
The End of Search

Google's Agentic Commerce Play Is the Biggest Structural Shift in Advertising Since the Click

The thing that happened quietly this week in marketing is going to matter more than almost anything else in the next two years. Google embedded ads directly inside AI Mode conversations. Not banners alongside the chat — sponsored placements that surface at the exact moment a user expresses purchase intent, inside the dialogue itself. They're calling it "Direct Offers." You tell the AI you're looking for running shoes, and a Reebok exclusive discount appears in the response. This is qualitatively different from search advertising.

Here's the structural shift: the traditional advertising funnel assumed a user would conduct a search, scan a results page, click a link, browse a site, and eventually convert. Each of those steps was a hand-off — and a data-collection opportunity — for brands. What Google just did collapses that funnel into a single conversational moment. The AI handles discovery, evaluation, and transaction facilitation in one exchange. The Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) they launched alongside this lets any AI agent — not just Google's — plug into retail inventory and payment rails using a standardized language. This is Google building infrastructure for a world where their Gemini assistant, OpenAI's GPT-based agents, and whatever Anthropic ships next are all routing commerce through the same protocol layer.

For someone heading into brand partnerships and marketing: the implication is that the creative brief is no longer primarily about attention capture. It's about intent interception. Brands that win in agentic commerce will be the ones whose product data, offer architecture, and conversational positioning are optimized for AI discovery — not just SEO. The new currency is structured product data, exclusive offer triggers, and brand voice that reads well in an AI response. The agencies that figure this out in the next 12 months will eat the ones that don't. This is the moment to understand the technology — not as a tool for writing copy faster, but as the medium itself changing underneath the entire industry.
