Today’s Rabbit Hole
Markets · Geopolitics · Energy
Hormuz Choke

The Strait of Hormuz Is the World's Most Important 21-Mile Bottleneck — Here's What Happens If It Stays Closed

The Strait of Hormuz is where the Persian Gulf meets the Gulf of Oman — a 21-mile-wide passage that about 17-21 million barrels of oil flow through every single day. That's roughly one-fifth of global oil consumption. Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Kuwait, UAE, and Iran all ship through it. There's no fully viable alternative. If it stays closed or meaningfully disrupted, the global energy market doesn't just tighten — it breaks.

Iran has threatened to close the Strait before — most notably in 2011-2012 during sanctions escalation — and markets flinched every time. But they never actually did it, and prices eventually normalized. This time feels different: Iran has declared the closure 'irreversible,' WTI is already at $115, and the Trump administration has publicly threatened to bomb power stations and bridges. The diplomatic off-ramps are narrowing. China and Russia are running interference at the UN. The Strait hasn't been physically closed in modern history — we're in unprecedented territory.

For Zubin specifically, this is a story that cuts across everything in your portfolio and career interests. On the markets side: energy stocks and GLD benefit directly; consumer discretionary (UBER, etc.) gets squeezed by fuel costs; tech stays relatively insulated but not immune to a broader macro shock. On the career side, if you're heading into sports/entertainment marketing in LA or NYC post-grad, understand that an inflationary shock from sustained $115+ oil compresses marketing budgets industry-wide. Brand spend gets cut when CFOs get nervous.

Watch: the UN Security Council vote today, whether Trump follows through on infrastructure strikes after the deadline passes, and how Gulf states (especially Saudi Arabia and UAE) respond. If Saudi production gets disrupted or damaged, Goldman's 22% S&P decline scenario becomes a real conversation. This is the macro story of 2026 — everything else is downstream of it.
