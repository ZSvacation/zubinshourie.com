Strait of Hormuz

Ceasefire Talks Move to Islamabad as Hormuz Remains a Choke Point — And China May Be Arming Iran

US Vice President JD Vance heads to Islamabad today for high-stakes negotiations with Iran's Foreign Minister Araghchi and parliamentary speaker Ghalibaf. The two-week ceasefire remains in place, but 'fragile' is doing a lot of work in that sentence — Iran has already set preconditions including a Lebanon ceasefire and release of frozen assets, and the Strait of Hormuz is still functioning as an Iranian-controlled bottleneck rather than open international waters.

The wildcard: US intelligence reportedly shows China is preparing to supply MANPADS air defense systems to Iran. That would put Beijing in a complicated position — it was credited with helping broker this very ceasefire. If confirmed, it poisons the diplomatic well fast and could push crude prices higher at exactly the wrong time.

The market read-through is stagflation risk. JPMorgan has flagged gas above $5/gallon if conflict continues; March CPI already showed a 10.9% jump in energy costs. Gold is approaching $4,800. IMF's Q2 global growth outlook sits at 3.3% — penciled in before things got worse.

Federal Court Questions Legal Basis for Trump's 10% Global Tariffs

A three-judge panel at the US Court of International Trade is grilling whether a persistent trade deficit alone legally justifies emergency tariffs. The Supreme Court already struck down an earlier IEEPA-based round in February — this current 10% blanket levy expires July 24 and now faces its own constitutional headwind. If struck down, it reshuffles the entire trade policy deck heading into summer.

Markets / April 10 / Macro Data

March CPI Surges 0.9% on Energy Shock — But Core Comes In Soft at 2.6%

Headline inflation jumped sharply in March, almost entirely on the back of a 10.9% energy spike tied to the Hormuz disruption. The relief: core CPI printed 0.2% MoM and 2.6% YoY, below forecasts — suggesting the Fed doesn't have a consumer price spiral on its hands yet, just an oil shock. That buys the Fed some cover to hold rates rather than hike, which explains why futures didn't fully collapse on the print.

Today’s Rabbit Hole
Sports Business · AI · Media Rights
AI Rewires Sports

AI Is Quietly Eating the Sports Media Business — And It's Moving Faster Than the Leagues Realize

The sports media landscape is in the middle of a structural shift that most fans don't notice but every executive is quietly panicking about. Streaming platforms and tech companies have been steadily acquiring live sports rights — the NFL, NBA, and college football have all done deals with Amazon, Apple, and Google in recent years. But the next wave isn't just about who owns the rights. It's about who controls the viewing experience, and AI is the variable that changes everything.

What's happening now: AI is enabling hyper-personalized broadcast feeds. Think adaptive overlays — tactical analysis for the hardcore fan, simplified stats for the casual viewer, real-time multilingual commentary for global audiences, all from the same game feed. Companies like Magnifi.AI and SportAI are selling these capabilities to mid-tier leagues that couldn't previously afford the production infrastructure. The democratization of premium sports production is compressing the competitive advantage that ESPN and Turner Sports have held for decades.

For someone heading into sports marketing and entertainment, here's the so-what: the jobs that are safe are the ones that require human relationships — sponsorship sales, talent representation, brand partnerships, event marketing. The jobs that are evaporating are commodity production and distribution roles. The strategic opportunity is in understanding AI well enough to deploy it in client-facing work — using AI-generated content and analytics to pitch sponsors, build fan engagement campaigns, or package talent deals with data-driven ROI arguments that weren't possible three years ago.

Watch Anthropic's 'Project Glasswing' and Meta's 'Muse Spark' rollout over the next 90 days. Both represent AI ecosystems that will plug directly into how media companies create and distribute content. The teams that figure out how to build on top of these platforms — rather than compete with them — will own the next decade of sports media.
