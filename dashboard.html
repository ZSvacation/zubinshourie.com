Today’s Rabbit Hole
Advertising · AI · Infrastructure
Ads Inside AI

OpenAI Is Selling Ads in ChatGPT. Meta Wants to Automate Every Ad. Google Is Monetizing AI Search. The Advertising Stack Is Being Rebuilt.

This is the week it became undeniable: AI is not just changing how ads are targeted — it’s changing where ads live. OpenAI launched sponsored responses inside ChatGPT at $60 CPM, with brands like Best Buy, Target, and Expedia as launch partners. That’s a new surface that didn’t exist six months ago, inserted directly into conversational interactions with hundreds of millions of users.

Meta announced plans to fully automate advertising by end of 2026. The vision: brands provide product assets and a budget, and Meta’s AI handles targeting, creative assembly, placement, and optimization. No media buyers. No manual A/B tests. Just inputs and outcomes. Google, meanwhile, has pushed AI Mode to 75 million users and is threading ads into its AI-generated search results — with 17% of citations pointing back to Google-owned properties, raising publisher concerns.

For someone going into marketing, this is the ground shifting beneath the industry. The skill set that mattered five years ago — manual campaign management, keyword bidding, creative brief writing — is being compressed into AI workflows. What emerges on the other side likely rewards strategic thinking, brand positioning, and the ability to work with AI systems rather than against them. The $1.3 trillion global ad market is growing 9.1% this year. The question is who captures it and how.
