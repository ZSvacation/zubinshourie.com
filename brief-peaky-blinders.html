Peaky Blinders: The Immortal Man — Tommy Shelby Returns for the Final Chapter

Netflix · Directed by Tom Harper · Written by Steven Knight

Six seasons of Peaky Blinders ended in 2022 with Tommy Shelby's fate deliberately left open. Four years later, Cillian Murphy is back — and the story has been given an unusual closing chapter: a standalone feature film set during World War II, not a seventh season. The shift makes sense thematically. The TV show tracked Tommy's rise from post-WWI gangster to legitimate political force. The film brings him full circle, setting his final story inside a second world war, haunted by his first one.

What the Film Is About

Tommy Shelby is living in a rural house, writing a novel, still haunted by the ghost of his deceased daughter Ruby. He's been in self-imposed exile — estranged from his son and the world that made him. But he gets pulled back in when his son becomes entangled in a Nazi plot. Nazi Germany has been using Jewish concentration camp labor to produce counterfeit British pound notes — an actual historical operation called Operation Bernhard — designed to collapse the British economy by flooding it with fake currency. Tommy has to return to Birmingham, navigate wartime London, and take on enemies with a completely different kind of power than he's faced before.

"It's the closing of a loop that started in the trenches of WWI. The Peaky Blinders universe has always been a war story — now it's admitting it directly."

New Cast, Familiar Stakes

Rebecca Ferguson and Barry Keoghan join the cast for the film, alongside returning series regulars. Director Tom Harper — who helmed some of the show's best episodes — returns to close out the story. The film premiered in Birmingham (fitting) on March 2, had a limited cinema run from March 6, and streams globally on Netflix on March 20.

Early reviews are mixed-positive. Critics praise the visual style, Murphy's performance, and the atmospheric period detail, but note the film struggles slightly with pacing and underdeveloped supporting characters — common trade-offs when condensing a TV-scale story into feature format. For fans of the show, it's a send-off worth watching. For newcomers, start with the series first.
