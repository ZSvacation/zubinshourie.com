The 2026 Oscars Are Four Days Away — Here's What You Need to Know

The Daily Brief · March 11, 2026

The 98th Academy Awards air Sunday, March 15 at the Dolby Theatre in Hollywood. Conan O'Brien hosts. It's his first time hosting the Oscars, and after years of ceremony hosts who either played it too safe or overcorrected into cringe, O'Brien feels like a genuinely interesting pick — someone who's good at reading a room and can improvise his way through the night without needing a teleprompter.

Best Picture: Wide Open

Going into the weekend, three films are still legitimately in contention for Best Picture:

Sinners (Ryan Coogler) has been the emotional frontrunner since its debut. A blues-era Mississippi story with horror undertones, it's the kind of original, auteur-driven work that the Academy has increasingly rewarded in the post-Parasite era. It has the most critics' group wins of the season and probably the most passionate advocates in the voting body.

One Battle After Another (Paul Thomas Anderson) is the prestige anchor of the year — a sprawling, technically immaculate adaptation of Thomas Pynchon's Bleeding Edge, set in post-9/11 New York. It's the kind of film the Academy loves on paper but sometimes fails to reward in practice. PTA has been nominated eight times and won once for The Phantom Thread (screenplay). This might finally be his night.

Marty Supreme (Harmony Korine) is the wild card. A table tennis biopic starring Timothée Chalamet, shot in a grainy, propulsive style that feels like nothing else in recent memory. It has the most divided reception — some voters love it, some find it grating — which makes its actual ceiling hard to predict.

"Michael B. Jordan is the surprise frontrunner for Best Actor after winning the Actor Awards over both Chalamet and DiCaprio."

Best Actor: The Biggest Surprise of the Season

The Best Actor race was supposed to be a two-horse race between Timothée Chalamet (Marty Supreme) and Leonardo DiCaprio (One Battle After Another). Then the Actor Awards happened. Michael B. Jordan won for his performance in Sinners — playing a blues musician who makes a deal he shouldn't have — and since then momentum has been building his way. He's now the narrowest of favorites.

If Jordan wins, it would be his first Oscar nomination and win simultaneously, only the 14th time that's happened in Academy history. It would also represent a clean sweep for Sinners in two of the major categories, which could pull Best Picture its way as well.

The Rest of the Major Categories

Best Actress looks like Cate Blanchett's for The Hours remake, a role that's generated unanimous praise and no real challengers since September. Best Director is probably PTA's to lose — the DGA gave it to him, and that correlation holds more often than not. Best Animated Feature is Pixar's Elio, which was never seriously challenged.

Best International Film has the most interesting race: the front-runner is The Seed of the Sacred Fig from Iran (an irony given current events), directed by Mohammad Rasoulof, who filmed it secretly and escaped Iran before its premiere at Cannes. The film is about a Revolutionary Court investigator whose family starts to question everything he stands for. It's a genuinely important work, and winning would carry obvious symbolic weight this weekend.
